What is calibration?

A well-calibrated person's stated confidence matches reality: when they say they're 70% sure, they're right about 70% of the time — no more, no less. This matters because overconfidence leads to poor decisions while underconfidence means leaving good options on the table. Good forecasters, scientists, and decision-makers all cultivate this skill. Julia Galef calls it the Scout Mindset — caring more about what is true than about being right.

This quiz presents 40 true/false statements. For each one, pick your answer and set how confident you are (50% = just guessing, 100% = certain). At the end you'll see a calibration curve and a score showing how well your confidence tracked your actual accuracy.
